Book Recomendations for Adults

Mystery & Thriller

Previous Next






































What Happened to Nina? by Dervla McTiernan Nina, a college student, vanishes after a weekend trip with her boyfriend. His version of events doesn’t match what her family believes, and soon the case becomes a media spectacle. McTiernan explores how truth can be manipulated, how families cope with uncertainty, and how public opinion can shape justice. It’s a gripping psychological thriller that asks whether we can ever truly know what happened when stories conflict.


The Last Devil to Die by Richard Osman The beloved Thursday Murder Club returns as the group investigates the death of an antiques dealer. What begins as a small mystery spirals into a web of smuggling, fraud, and hidden identities. Osman balances humor with suspense, offering witty banter between his elderly sleuths while keeping readers hooked with clever twists. It’s both a cozy mystery and a sharp commentary on crime and community.

Science-Fiction and Fantasy

Previous Next






































The Terraformers by Annalee Newitz In a distant future, a team of environmental engineers is tasked with terraforming a planet for corporate profit. But when they uncover secrets about the planet’s inhabitants and ecosystems, they must decide whether to protect life or serve their employers. The novel blends hard science fiction with social commentary, exploring themes of ecology, capitalism, and identity. It’s imaginative, thought-provoking, and deeply relevant to our own environmental challenges.


House of Sky and Breath by Sarah J. Maas The second book in the Crescent City series continues Bryce Quinlan’s journey as she and Hunt Athalar face new threats to their world. Packed with romance, political intrigue, and explosive battles, the story expands the universe with new alliances and betrayals. Maas weaves together themes of love, loyalty, and rebellion, creating a sweeping fantasy epic that appeals to fans of both romance and adventure.

Romance

Previous Next






































Funny Story by Emily Henry Daphne’s fiancé leaves her for another woman, and she ends up living with her ex’s new girlfriend’s ex-boyfriend. What starts as a tense arrangement slowly turns into a charming romance filled with banter, vulnerability, and healing. Henry’s trademark humor and emotional depth shine as she explores how love can grow in unexpected places and how heartbreak can lead to new beginnings.


The Rom-Commers by Katherine Center Emma, a struggling screenwriter, is hired to fix a rom-com script for a famous but cynical writer. As they clash over what makes a good love story, Emma finds herself living out her own romantic comedy. Center blends humor with heartfelt emotion, showing how optimism and vulnerability can transform even the most jaded hearts. It’s a joyful celebration of love and storytelling.
