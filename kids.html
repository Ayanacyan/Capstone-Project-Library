Book Recomendations for Kids below 5

Humor and fun

Previous Next






































Grumpy Monkey – Jim Panzee wakes up feeling grumpy, though he doesn’t know why. His friends try to cheer him up with advice—smiling, dancing, eating bananas—but nothing works. Eventually, Jim realizes it’s okay to feel grumpy sometimes, and that emotions don’t need fixing right away. The book uses humor to normalize feelings and reassure kids that moods are natural.


Don’t Let the Pigeon Drive the Bus! – A bus driver leaves and asks readers to keep an eye on things. The pigeon immediately begs, pleads, and bargains to drive the bus, using silly arguments and dramatic antics. The interactive format makes children laugh while empowering them to say “no.” It’s a playful introduction to boundaries and decision-making.


Poo in the Zoo – Zookeeper Bob encounters all sorts of animal droppings—big, small, smelly, and strange. The rhyming text and silly illustrations delight children with gross-out humor, while a twist ending introduces a magical creature whose glittery poop becomes a hit. It’s lighthearted fun that makes kids giggle while sneaking in rhythm and rhyme.

Fantasy

Previous Next






































Where the Wild Things Are – Max, a mischievous boy dressed in a wolf costume, gets sent to his room after causing trouble. In his imagination, he sails away to a land of Wild Things—giant, strange creatures who crown him their king. Though he enjoys the power and freedom, Max eventually feels lonely and realizes that love and home matter more than ruling. It’s a story about imagination, emotions, and the comfort of returning to family.


Room on the Broom – A friendly witch and her cat fly through the sky, but as her belongings fall, different animals (a dog, a bird, a frog) help retrieve them and ask for a ride. The broom becomes crowded, but when a dragon threatens the witch, the animals band together to save her. The tale celebrates teamwork, kindness, and the joy of sharing space with friends.


The Gruffalo – A clever little mouse walks through the forest, inventing a scary creature called the Gruffalo to frighten predators like a fox, owl, and snake. To his surprise, the Gruffalo turns out to be real! The mouse must use his wit again to outsmart the Gruffalo himself. It’s a playful story about brains over brawn and the power of imagination.

Classic Picture Books

Previous Next






































Goodnight Moon – A little bunny says goodnight to everything in his room—the red balloon, the kittens, the mittens—in a soothing rhythm. The gentle repetition and soft illustrations create a calming bedtime ritual, helping children wind down before sleep.


The Tale of Peter Rabbit – Peter, a curious rabbit, sneaks into Mr. McGregor’s garden despite his mother’s warning. He eats vegetables, gets chased, and barely escapes. The story blends adventure with a moral lesson about listening to parents and the consequences of mischief.


Corduroy – Corduroy is a teddy bear in a department store who longs for a home. When a little girl wants to buy him, her mother says no because he’s missing a button. That night, Corduroy searches the store for his button, but the next day, the girl returns and buys him anyway. It’s a touching story about belonging, love, and acceptance.
